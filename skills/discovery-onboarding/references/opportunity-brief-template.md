### Partnerships ➡ Delivery Handoff Notes

Write a comprehensive, client-facing Delivery Handoff Notes that produces both a short narrative and a bulleted “Key Highlights” section, in American English. Aim for the same level of detail as the example below. Cover at minimum:

- Context of scope changes since proposal sign-off and kickoff, including quick-start decisions and expanded team participation.
- New stakeholders and roles (names and titles) added by the client.
- Clear definitions of deployment milestones (Teaser/Pre‑purchase Flow and MVP/Intake Survey/Purchase Flow), their goals, success criteria, and intended outcomes.
- Working approach (timeboxing, iterative/agile rollout, month‑to‑month extensions, and pre‑approval process for changes).
- Brand and naming considerations (interim brand elements, ownership of renaming decision, and expected decision timeline).
- Technical leadership and backend staffing needs, integrations planning, and potential blockers.
- Risks and dependencies that could impact timeline, with mitigation notes.
- Summary of the revised SOW: timeline, hours by role, and expected cost.
- Governance, approvals, and next steps (what happens immediately after handoff).

Then add a “Key Highlights” bullet list that summarizes: the two milestones with durations, kickoff activities (roadmap analysis, iterative rollout, risk evaluation, target dates), technical lead hours, dual design tracks, post‑teaser sprints for MVP features, added hours for sprints, total expected timeline, total squad hours, total expected cost, and collaboration/review cadence.

If specific details are still TBD, include clear placeholders in brackets (e.g., [Feature TBD], [Name], [Duration], [Cost estimate]). Keep formatting consistent, numbers explicit (e.g., “Total expected timeline: 12 weeks”), and language clear and actionable.
